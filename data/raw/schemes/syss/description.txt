The name of this welfare scheme is Skilled Youth Startup Scheme. It is also known as SYSS. The &amp;quot;Skilled Youth Start-Up Scheme (SYSS)&amp;quot; was launched with effect on 15th August 2020 by the Commerce &amp;amp; Industries Department of the Government of Sikkim, with the vision to reduce unemployment and create adequate self-employment opportunities in the long run. SYSS is intended to generate equitable entrepreneurial opportunities in rural as well as urban areas of Sikkim, particularly among the educated unemployed youth to become self-employed by setting up Manufacturing / Service / Business / Co-Operative / Agriculture &amp;amp; Allied Activities by availing loans (category wise) with back ended subsidy assistance from the State Government @ 50% for Below Poverty Line (BPL) and @ 35% for rest on financially viable/ bankable Projects cost.
&lt;br&gt;

Objectives:
1.  To promote entrepreneurial qualities amongst the local unemployed youth and encourage them for setting up any commercially viable/ bankable business venture in any sector including Agriculture / Horticulture / Food Processing / Animal Husbandry / Handloom &amp;amp; Handicrafts / Retail / Tourism / Services / Manufacturing etc. in the State in tune with the State Government policies. 
2.  To generate equitable entrepreneurial opportunities in rural as well as urban areas of the State through the setting up of new self-employment ventures/projects by providing one-time non-refundable financial assistance of 50% / 35% with back-ended subsidy on project approval by the Bank/ PSU financial institution.
&lt;br&gt;

The scheme will be implemented by the Department of Commerce &amp;amp; Industries, Govt. of Sikkim, through the District Industries Centres (DICs) for appraisal of the projects, their financial viability and providing assistance for their credit linkage with the financial institutions and line Departments. The scheme targets beneficiaries such as Individual, Industries. It is specifically implemented in Sikkim. The implementing agency is The Department Of Commerce & Industries Through The District Industries Centres (DICs). Additionally, the nodal department is Commerce & Industries Department.

It falls under the categories: Business & Entrepreneurship, Agriculture,Rural & Environment, Skills & Employment.

Eligibility criteria include: Eligibility of the Applicant:
The applicant should possess a Certificate of Identification (COI) of self or his/her 
father possessing Sikkim Subject Certificate / Certificate of Identification / Residential Certificate for urban areas.
The applicant should be unemployed possessing a minimum educational qualification 
of class 5th from any recognized school. For technical projects/ manufacturing and service sector the applicants should possess a certificate from Technical Institute.
Applicant should be in the age group between 18 years to 45 years of age on the date of filing the application.
Only one member of a family shall be eligible to avail of the benefits under the Scheme.
The family income shall not exceed ₹ 8,00,000 per annum. The ‘family’ includes self, spouse, and kids in the case of married applicant. In the case of unmarried beneficiary, the family includes parents and unmarried siblings. In the case of Government Servant, ‘C’ and ‘D’ employees&amp;#39; children can only avail of this benefit.
The scheme comprises of subsidy and contribution of the beneficiary at 5 % of the project cost.
The beneficiary should not be a defaulter to any nationalized bank / financial institution / Cooperative bank.
A person who has already availed of subsidy under CMSS/ PMEGP shall not be eligible under the scheme.
Trade License is mandatory for all kinds of projects/businesses.
The applicant falling under the Persons with Disabilities (PWD) category will be given priority and can avail 50% loan subsidy.
The Project cost will include Capital Expenditure and three months working capital requirements for the venture depending upon the project. 
The Cost of the land should not be included in the Project cost. The cost of the readybuilt, as well as long lease or rental Work shed/ Workshop/ Shop, can be included in the project cost subject to restricting such cost of readybuilt as well as long lease or rental Work shed/ workshop for a maximum period of 2 years only.
&lt;br&gt;
