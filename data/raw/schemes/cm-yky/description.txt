The name of this welfare scheme is Chief Minister’s Yuva Kaushal Yojana. It is also known as CM YKY. The scheme “Chief Minister’s Yuva Kaushal Yojana (CM YKY)” has been launched in the state of Arunachal Pradesh with effect from 1 April 2018 to provide high-end training to youth across the country. The programme is fully funded under the state plan with a specific focus on providing job linked high end training programme to the youth. The objective of the scheme is to provide high-end training to the youth across the state to enable them to find jobs in the private sector.
Important Features: There are 2 categories of training provided under CM YKY:
1.	Training within the state by inviting reputed Vocation Training Providers and providing them with suitable incentives.
2.	Training outside the State by identifying high-end reputed Vocational Training Providers and courses. The scheme targets beneficiaries such as Individual. It is specifically implemented in Arunachal Pradesh. The implementing agency is Skill Centres Under CM Kaushal Vikas Yojna. Additionally, the nodal department is Skill Development and Entrepreneurship Department.

It falls under the categories: Skills & Employment.

Eligibility criteria include: The applicant should be a permanent resident of Arunachal Pradesh.
The applicant should have a minimum qualification of class X.
