The name of this welfare scheme is General Poor Class Scholarship (Class 6 to 8). It is also known as GPCS. The &amp;quot;General Poor Class Scholarship (Class 6 to 8)&amp;quot; scheme, launched by the School Education Department, Government of Madhya Pradesh, aims to motivate and economically support students from lower economic backgrounds. Under this scheme, scholarships are provided to students studying in Classes 6 to 8 in government schools. Students whose annual family income is less than ₹1,00,000/- and who belong to the Below Poverty Line (BPL) category are eligible for the benefits. The scheme targets beneficiaries such as Individual. It is specifically implemented in Madhya Pradesh. The implementing agency is School Education Department, Government Of Madhya Pradesh. Additionally, the nodal department is School Education Department.

The scheme provides the following benefits: Girl students will receive a scholarship of ₹300/per year.
  Boy students will receive a scholarship of ₹200/per year.

Note: The scholarship amount is directly transferred to the student&amp;#39;s bank account.

Eligibility criteria include: The applicant should be a permanent resident of Madhya Pradesh.

   The student should belong to the Below Poverty Line (BPL) category.
   The student&amp;#39;s parents&amp;#39; total income from all sources should be less than ₹1,00,000/per year.
   The student should have passed the previous class. The application process is as follows: To avail of the benefits under this scheme, eligible students can contact the Principal of their respective government schools/institutions.
