The name of this welfare scheme is Farmers Interest Group (FIG). It is also known as FIG. The “Farmers Interest Group (FIG)” scheme, by the Agriculture-Farmers Welfare Department, Tamil Nadu, supports every group of 15 farmers with grants for agricultural development. The scheme offers financial assistance for purchasing office equipment, training, ID cards, and district-level meetings.
&lt;br&gt; The scheme targets beneficiaries such as Registered Societies, Joint Liability Groups (JLGS). It is specifically implemented in Tamil Nadu. Additionally, the nodal department is Agriculture Department.

The scheme provides the following benefits: Office Equipment: ₹5,000/per group for purchasing office equipment, technical bulletins, and setting up a library.  Training and Skill Acquisition: ₹4000/per group for training and skill acquisition by farmer members.  ID Cards: ₹400/per group for issuing ID cards to members.  DistrictLevel Meetings: ₹20,000/per group for conducting districtlevel meetings.

Eligibility criteria include: The beneficiary should be a Farmer.
  The beneficiary should be a resident of Tamil Nadu.
  The beneficiary should be part of a Farmer’s group of 15 farmers. The application process is as follows: Step 1: Collect the application form from the nearest Agricultural Office.
Step 2: Fill out the application form with the required details and attach mandatory documents (selfattested, if required). 
Step 3: Submit the form to the concerned office:
&gt; Village Level: Submit the application to the Assistant Agricultural Officer.

&gt; Block Level: Submit to the Agricultural Officer or Deputy Agricultural Officer.

&gt; District Level: Submit to the Joint Director of Agriculture.
