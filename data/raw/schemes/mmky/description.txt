The name of this welfare scheme is Mukhya Mantri Kanyadaan Yojana. It is also known as MMKY. The Mukhya Mantri Kanyadan Yojana in Himachal Pradesh is a welfare scheme aimed at providing financial assistance for the marriage of daughters from economically weaker sections of society. Under this scheme, daughters of families below the poverty line, widows, destitute women, and families with an annual income of less than Rs. 35,000 are eligible for financial assistance from the government for their marriage. The scheme aims to reduce the financial burden on families during the marriage of their daughters and promote gender equality. The scheme has been successful in providing financial support to many families, particularly those from underprivileged backgrounds, and helping them to celebrate the marriage of their daughters with dignity and joy. The scheme targets beneficiaries such as Individual. It is specifically implemented in Himachal Pradesh. Additionally, the nodal department is Health and Family Welfare Department, Himachal Pradesh.

It falls under the categories: Social welfare & Empowerment, Women and Child.

Eligibility criteria include: Daughters of families below the poverty line.
Widows and destitute women.
