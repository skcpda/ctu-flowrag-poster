The name of this welfare scheme is Nalkoop Khanan Yojana- Madhya Pradesh. It is also known as NKYMP. The &amp;quot;Nalkoop Khanan Yojana&amp;quot; was launched on 31st December 2001, by the Farmer Welfare and Agriculture Development Department, Government of Madhya Pradesh, to increase the irrigation area by harnessing underground water. Under this scheme, farmers from Scheduled Caste and Scheduled Tribe categories are eligible for a subsidy covering 75% of the unit cost (up to ₹25,000/-) for digging a tube well and 75% of the unit cost (up to ₹15,000/-) for the successful installation of a tube well submersible pump. This scheme is effective in entire Madhya Pradesh, except in Shajapur and Indore. The scheme targets beneficiaries such as Individual. It is specifically implemented in Madhya Pradesh. The implementing agency is Farmer Welfare And Agriculture Development Department, Government Of Madhya Pradesh. Additionally, the nodal department is Farmer Welfare and Agriculture Development Department.

The scheme provides the following benefits: &gt; Subsidy for Tube Well Digging:


  A grant of 75% of the unit cost (up to a maximum of ₹25,000/), is provided for digging a tube well.
  Applicable for both successful and unsuccessful digging.

&gt; Subsidy for Submersible Pump Installation:


  A grant of 75% of the unit cost (up to a maximum of ₹15,000/), is provided for the successful installation of a submersible pump on a tube well.
  Applicable only for successful installation of a Submersible Pump.

Note: The subsidy is transferred to the bank account of the farmers.

Eligibility criteria include: The applicant should be a native of Madhya Pradesh.
   The applicant should be a farmer.
   Farmers from Shajapur and Indore districts of Madhya Pradesh are excluded from this scheme.
   The applicant should belong to the Scheduled Caste or Scheduled Tribe category.
   The applicant should own agricultural land within the state. The application process is as follows: Eligible farmers can apply for benefits under the scheme by visiting the office of the Deputy Director, Farmers Welfare and Agriculture Development Department in their respective district in Madhya Pradesh.
