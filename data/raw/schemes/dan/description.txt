The name of this welfare scheme is Delhi Arogya Nidhi. It is also known as DAN. The &amp;quot;Delhi Arogya Nidhi (DAN)&amp;quot; is a scheme implemented by the Directorate General of Health Services to provide financial assistance of up to ₹1.5 lacs to needy patients who have National Food Security Cards for treatment of diseases in Government hospitals only. The scheme targets beneficiaries such as Individual. It is specifically implemented in Delhi. The implementing agency is Directorate General Of Health Services, Government NCT Of Delhi. Additionally, the nodal department is Health & Family Welfare.

The scheme provides the following benefits: Under the scheme, financial assistance of up to ₹5 lacs will be provided to needy patients who have National Food Security Cards for treatment of diseases in Government hospitals only.

Eligibility criteria include: Patients should have a National Food Security Card.
The patient must be a resident of Delhi and has to furnish domicile proof of residing in Delhi continuously for the last 3 years (prior to the date of submission of the application).
Treatment should be from the Government Hospital in Delhi. The application process is as follows: Step 01: Visit the official website to download the Application Form for Financial Aid.
Step 02: Fill out the form with all requisites details.
Step 03: The photocopies of all documents are to be attached with the application and the original is to be brought at the time of submission of same for verification.
Step 04: Applicant has to submit an undertaking for his signature verification as given in the application form.
