The name of this welfare scheme is Rajasthan Chief Minister's Relief Fund - Medical Help. It is also known as RCMRF-MH. Rajasthan Chief Minister’s Relief Fund – medical help scheme launched on April 1999 under Chief Minister’s Relief Fund in Rajasthan. Dependents of untimely dead due to famine, floods, excessive rains, fires and accidental accidents help.


For treatment information - Assistance is accepted in recognized private hospitals from the major Government/ Chief Minister’s Relief Fund of the state. 40% maximum amount of rs1.50 lakh in government hospitals and 30% maximum amount of Rs 90,000 in recognized private hospitals, whichever is less, is accepted and sent to the concerned hospital. The scheme targets beneficiaries such as Individual. It is specifically implemented in Rajasthan. Additionally, the nodal department is Medical, Health and Family Welfare Department.

It falls under the categories: Health & Wellness.

Eligibility criteria include: The applicant should be Non BPL
The Annal Income of the Family should be greater than 2 LPA
Not be listed in ABMGRSBY (Ayushman Bharat Mahatma Gandhi Rajasthan Swasthya Bima Yojana) For more information, please refer to: Rajasthan Government Portal: https://jankalyan.rajasthan.gov.in/#/scheme/detail/434 Tags associated with the scheme include: Hospital, Medical Help, Non BPL, Rajasthan Scheme, Relief Fund.
