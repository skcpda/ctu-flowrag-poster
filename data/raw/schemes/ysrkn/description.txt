The name of this welfare scheme is YSR Kapu Nestham. It is also known as YSRKN. The scheme &amp;quot;YSR Kapu Nestham&amp;quot; is a Social Empowerment Scheme by the Backward Classes Welfare(C) Department, Govt. of Andhra Pradesh. The objective of the scheme is to financially empower Kapu, Balija, Telaga, Ontari women between the age of 45-60 years by enhancing their livelihood opportunities and living standards with the financial assistance of a total amount of ₹ 75,000/- over the period of 5 years i.e., ₹ 15,000/- per annum. The applicant should be a permanent resident of Andhra Pradesh. The scheme targets beneficiaries such as Individual. It is specifically implemented in Andhra Pradesh. Additionally, the nodal department is Dept of Social Welfare, WCD.

It falls under the categories: Social welfare & Empowerment, Women and Child.

Eligibility criteria include: Citizenship: The applicant should be a permanent resident of Andhra Pradesh.
Total Family Income: Total family Income of the applicant should be less than Rs.10,000/per month in Rural areas and Rs.12,000/per month in Urban areas.
Total family Land Holding: Total land holding of the family of the applicant should be less than 00 Acres of Wet Land OR 10 Acres of Dry Land OR 10 Acres of Land put together for this purpose.
Government Employee/Pensioner: No family member of the applicant should be a government employee or Government pensioner. The families of Sanitary workers are exempted.
FourWheeler: The family of the applicant should not own a fourwheeler (Taxis, Tractors, and Autos are exempted).
Electricity Consumption: Monthly electricity consumption of the family dwelling unit (own/rent) of the applicant should be less than 300 units.
Income Tax: No family member of the applicant should be an income tax payee.
Municipal Property: Less than 750 sq. feet of builtup area (Residential or commercial).
Age &amp; Gender: The applicant should be between 45 – 60 Years. The applicant should be a Female.
Category: The applicant must belong to the Kapu Community (Kapu, Balija, Telaga, or Ontari).
