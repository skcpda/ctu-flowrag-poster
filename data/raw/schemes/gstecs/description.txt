The name of this welfare scheme is GST Exemption Certificate Scheme. It is also known as GSTECS. The &quot;GST Exemption Certificate Scheme&quot; has been introduced by the Department of Heavy Industries, Ministry of Heavy Industries for issuing certificates to Persons with Disabilities (PwDs) having a disability percentage equal to or greater than 40%. The scheme provides GSC Concession to orthopedically disabled persons on purchase of a car. The scheme targets beneficiaries such as Individual. Additionally, the nodal ministry is Ministry of Heavy Industries and the nodal department is Department Of Heavy Industry.

The scheme provides the following benefits: A concession of 10% on GST can be availed through this certificate and zero cess would be applicable. A person availing of this certificate will have to pay 18% GST &amp; no cess instead of 28% GST &amp; applicable cess on the purchase of a car. 
The certificate is issued within a month of receipt of the application complete in all respects.
The certificate is valid for a period of 6 months from the date of issuance.

Eligibility criteria include: Any Orthopedic Physically handicapped person having a permanent disability of 40% &amp; above can apply for the benefit under the scheme, irrespective of whether the applicant drives the vehicle himself or otherwise.
Cars (automatic or manual) having a length less than or equal to 4m and engine capacity of not more than 1200 cc (petrol, Liquefied Petroleum Gases (LPG) or compressed natural gas (CNG) or 1500 cc (diesel) qualify for purchase under this scheme.
The said Certificate for availing the GST concession would be valid for a period of three months from the date of issue of the certificate and the vehicle would have to be purchased by the applicant within this threemonth period to avail the concession.
