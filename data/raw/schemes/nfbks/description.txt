The name of this welfare scheme is Naye Fal Bagicho Ki Sthapana. It is also known as NFBKS. The &quot;Naye Fal Bagecho Ki Sthapana&quot; under the Agriculture Department of the Rajasthan government Increases farmers&#39; income by facilitating the cultivation of high-value fruit crops that have a potentially higher market demand and profitability compared to traditional crops. Extend financial support and subsidies to smallholder farmers to facilitate the establishment of new orchards, enabling them to access resources and inputs required for fruit cultivation.


> Important points


1. It will be mandatory to install drip plants in gardens. Fruit plants will be made available to the farmers only after the establishment of the drip plant.
1. Farmers of tribal areas will be exempted from the requirement of a drip irrigation system if they plant gardens in less than 0.4-hectare area.
1. NHB for the establishment of fruit orchards. The subsidy will be given on purchasing saplings from Accredited Nursery/Ramhans Nursery/Government Undertaking/Agricultural Universities/Agriculture Colleges/Semi-Government, Government of India institutions/Research Centers/Farm or any other government institution and planting a garden.
1. After physical verification, the grant amount will be paid directly into the farmer&#39;s bank account. The scheme targets beneficiaries such as Individual. It is specifically implemented in Rajasthan. Additionally, the nodal department is Agriculture Department.

The scheme provides the following benefits: For highvalue and common interval crops, the maximum grant amount is 50% of the unit cost, up to a maximum of Rs. 30,000/per hectare.
For intensive horticultural orchards, the maximum grant amount is 40% of the unit cost, up to a maximum of Rs. 40,000/per hectare.
A farmer will be given a grant for a minimum of 4 hectares and a maximum of 0 hectares area.
Small/marginal farmers are eligible for an additional 25% grant.

Eligibility criteria include: The applicant should be a farmer.
   The applicant should be a native of Rajasthan.
   The applicant must have agricultural land ownership and irrigation sources.
   Farmers with land between 4 and 4 hectares are eligible for the grant.
   For Scheduled Caste/Tribe farmers and tribal areas, the minimum area limit is 2 hectares. The application process is as follows: > Registration
